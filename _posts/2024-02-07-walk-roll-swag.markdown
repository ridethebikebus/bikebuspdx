Three times each school year (October, February, and May), Oregon Department of Transportation (ODOT) Safe Routes to School (SRTS) sponsors Walk+Roll days.

The idea is to ask schools to ask their community to make safer, healthier, and greener ways to get to school.

ODOT's preferred tool to support this is by giving schools swag to give out to kids and parents.

That alone says a lot, but the [actual swag speaks volumes](https://new.oregonscreen.com/).

Let's start with stickers. Kids love stickers! They seem to have truly magical powers.

So let's see what ODOT offers:

Who are these for? Did anyone at ODOT run this by a child?

Since 2019, ODOT SRTS has had official "Walk+Roll" mascots, *Strider and Viv.* My kids love graphic novels and comics. But can't we do better than this?


![panels from Strider and Viv comic](/assets/images/odot-srts-striver-viv.jpg)

Results this uninspired are only ever the output of intensly bureaucratic institutions far removed from the people they serves. In that way, these swag failures are not different from ODOT's larger safety and environmental failures: their focus is self-important and wasteful. This swag is about advertising ODOT, the same way ODOT builds more roads so that it can grow. Much of it goes unused, the same way most roads are massively underutilized.

Dear ODOT SRTS: If you want to incentivize kids walking and rolling, here is what would help:

- Stickers for things that kids like, which is almost anything, except what you've printed.
- Spoke cards kids can collect to celebrate their participation.
- Reflector keychains to attach to backpacks and jackets.
- Bubbles.
- Bike spoke lights/glow beads.
- Glow sticks.

This list goes on and on, you can visit a dentist's office or grocery store to see other examples of things kids look forward to.

We have another [Walk+Roll Day on May 8](https://www.oregonsaferoutes.org/may-challenge/). I'd love to see ODOT step up its game.

*Note for Bike/Walk Bus organizers: you may want to look at Metro's [Regional Travel Options grants](https://www.oregonmetro.gov/tools-partners/grants-and-resources/regional-travel-options-program), which can pay for things like incentives.*
